What I see different — plain-English summary

In scope (feature-card section)

  • 1280 desktop: live now renders three feature cards in a single row, matching the preview and the brief. Cards land at left positions 71, 480, 889 px (each 305 px wide) — three columns confirmed.
  • 768 tablet: live renders 2 cards on row 1 (cards 01 + 02) and 1 card on row 2 (card 03), matching the brief's 2-column responsive spec. Note the static preview at 768 actually stacks all three cards in a single column; this is a preview/brief contradiction. The brief and the issue both say 2-column at 768, and the live correctly follows the brief.
  • 375 mobile: live stacks all three cards in a single column, matching the preview and the brief.
  • The live cards are visually taller than the preview cards in every viewport because the live cards include richer chrome (top kicker bar, "01 / TOOLS"-style eyebrows, corner arrow icons) and the preview HTML is a simpler mockup without that chrome. This drives the per-section pixel deltas (7–11%); it is a content-height delta, not a layout problem.
  • No horizontal overflow at any viewport (live document widths: 1265 / 753 / 360 — all under their viewport widths).

Out of scope (other sub-cycles, expected DELTA)

  • Header / nav (sub-cycle 8.1).
  • Hero whitespace and CTA spacing at 1280 (sub-cycle 8.5).
  • Footer CTA polish, FAQ icons, footer casing (sub-cycle 8.6).
  • These render the whole-page deltas at 44–55%, but contributing sections were not addressed in this sub-cycle and they must not block the 8.4 verdict.
