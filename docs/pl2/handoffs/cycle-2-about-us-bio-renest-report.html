What changed — plain-English summary

  • The bio "Who we are." block has been demoted from a separately-marked section to its proper home inside §C (Open source), per locked decision R9.
  • The dy-section--bio-block marker is gone from the rendered HTML (verified independently in the live DOM).
  • A 1 px horizontal hairline rule (token --theme-border-color: #E5E1DC) is visible immediately above the bio h3 at every viewport, matching the preview's separator treatment.
  • The bio h3 and body paragraph remain centered, max-width: 720px, margin-inline: auto — viewport-independent properties; same gestalt at 1280, 768, and 375.
  • The rendered live page output is visually equivalent to Cycle 1 at the pixel level — because, as F's structural finding documented, the bio was already inside §C in the Cycle 1 DOM; the marker class was a label, not a layout driver. Cycle 1's "promoted section" reading was based on the presence of the marker, not on a distinct standalone section element. With the marker now removed and the CSS selector rewritten to .dy-section--centered-white, R9 is satisfied semantically and the §C-tail row flips MATCH.
  • No regression on /services or /open-source-projects: F's broader replacement selector .dy-section--centered-white .dy-section__content > .grid-wrapper + .heading.h3 false-matches nothing on either sibling.
  • No new orphan words; "Who we are." renders on a single centered line at all viewports.
