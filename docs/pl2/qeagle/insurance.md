**Challenge:** Evolving compliance standards (IRDAI, HIPAA, PCI DSS) create audit risks.

**Solution:** Policy-driven test automation with built-in PII/PHI validations ensured zero nonconformities in pre-audit cycles across health and life insurance platforms.

FAQs – Insurance Software Testing

## Frequently Asked Qestions

[What is insurance software testing?](#collapse-14514dc69eeb07e5e2c6)

It involves validating the accuracy, security, and performance of digital insurance platforms, ensuring policy workflows, claims, and compliance are defect-free.

[Why is automation important in insurance testing?](#collapse-35c8fe469eeb07e5e2c6)

Insurance apps involve frequent regulatory updates, complex rule engines, and high-volume data—making automation essential for speed, consistency, and cost-efficiency.

[Which tools are used in insurance test automation?](#collapse-c29457c69eeb07e5e2c6)

* Functional/UI: Selenium, TestNG, Cypress
* API: Postman, REST Assured
* Performance: JMeter, LoadRunner
* DevOps: Jenkins, GitHub, Bamboo
* Data: SQL, Python ETL scripts

[Do you support test automation for legacy systems?](#collapse-f0f54c169eeb07e5e2c6)

Yes. We use API wrappers, RPA tools, and data simulation techniques to test legacy insurance platforms and integrate them with modern systems.

[What regulations must be considered?](#collapse-d9162c969eeb07e5e2c6)

* HIPAA for health insurance
* PCI DSS for payments
* SOC 2 for cloud security
* IRDAI and state-specific compliance mandates
