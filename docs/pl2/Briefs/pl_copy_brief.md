# Copy Brief

*Performant Labs — Style & Mechanic Rules. Updated 2026-04-26.*

Style and mechanic rules for Performant Labs copy. Positioning, voice, vocabulary, ICP, and messaging pillars live in `pl_brand_brief.md` — that document governs. When this guide and the brand brief disagree, the brand brief wins.

This is the *how-we-write* layer. It does not define who we are or what we say.

## Voice rules (non-negotiable)

### Tense

Present tense, active voice. Describe what happens now, not what could happen.

- ✓ "ATK heals broken tests in CI."
- ✗ "Broken tests can be healed by ATK."
- ✗ "Will help you ship."

### Casing

Sentence case everywhere. Only proper nouns get capitals. Headings, buttons, nav — all sentence case.

- ✓ "Book a testing review", "Call today", "How we do it"
- ✗ "Book A Testing Review", "CALL TODAY", "How We Do It"

### Length

Be brief. Cut adjectives. Two short sentences usually beat one long one. Headlines should fit in no more than two lines at typical hero widths — confident editorial typography wants size, not a single-line constraint. Subheads cap at two sentences. Don't pad to hit a length, and don't truncate past the point of clarity.

- ✗ "Performant Labs delivers cutting-edge, AI-powered, full-spectrum testing solutions for enterprise Drupal customers."

### Punctuation

Periods on fragments. Em dashes for asides. No exclamation points, ellipses, or semicolons.

- ✓ "Ship Drupal releases with confidence."
- ✓ "Tests heal themselves — and we run this nightly on our own site."
- ✗ "Ship with confidence!"
- ✗ "Tests heal themselves..."

### Emoji

Never. Not anywhere. No emoji in copy, buttons, nav, or messaging.

### Contractions

Use freely. They keep the voice human.

- ✓ "You're shipping faster."
- ✓ "Don't trust a flaky suite."

### Numbers

Numerals, not words.

- ✓ "19 years of Drupal experience", "65 SDC components"
- ✗ "nineteen years of Drupal experience"

## Words to avoid

- **Hype words:** "revolutionary," "game-changing," "cutting-edge," "next-gen."
- **Weasel words:** "may," "might," "could," "potentially" — be decisive.
- **Marketing jargon:** "synergy," "leverage," "paradigm shift," "disruptive."
- **Vague qualifiers:** "some," "many," "several" — be specific or omit.
- **Exclamation points:** they undermine certainty.

For the *positive* vocabulary lock — *testing engineer*, *Claude agent(s)*, ATK/Testor naming, *Playwright* before *Cypress*, *nearshore* not *offshore* — see `pl_brand_brief.md` §5.

## Button and CTA copy

Rules:

- Sentence case: "Book a testing review", not "Book A Testing Review" or "BOOK A TESTING REVIEW."
- Action verb + object: "Book a review", not just "Book."
- Short and direct: 1–4 words.
- Never use "Submit" or "OK" — too generic.
- Use second person where possible (the *you* is implied in the verb).

The list of approved CTAs for the current site is maintained per page in `phase-2-page-plan.md` and the `Existing Pages/` snapshots — refer to those for canonical button copy.

## Tone in action

### Confident & assertive (✓ correct)

> "Performant Labs built the Drupal testing stack the ecosystem already uses."

Direct statement of fact. No hedging.

### Brief & ruthless (✓ correct)

> "Ship Drupal releases with confidence."

One sentence. Essential information only.

### Hype & marketing jargon (✗ wrong)

> "Revolutionize your entire web infrastructure with cutting-edge AI-powered optimization that leverages next-generation machine learning!"

All hype, no substance. Too many empty words.

### Uncertain & weasel words (✗ wrong)

> "Performant Labs may help detect potential issues that could improve your testing workflow."

"May," "could," "potentially" undermine certainty.

### Vague & generic (✗ wrong)

> "Our solution helps teams with their testing needs."

"Solution," "teams," "needs" — too abstract. Say what you do.

## Accessibility in copy

### Keep it scannable

- Short paragraphs (2–3 sentences max).
- Subheadings to break up sections.
- Bullet lists instead of long paragraphs.
- Reasonable line length (50–80 characters ideal).

### Avoid jargon

Define technical terms on first use. Assume the reader is smart but may not know your stack.

> "ATK (Automated Testing Kit) is the open-source Drupal test framework we maintain on drupal.org."

### Use plain language

- Prefer short words: "use" over "utilize," "help" over "facilitate."
- Use active voice: "we detect regressions" not "regressions are detected."
- Be concrete: "19 years of Drupal experience" not "deep Drupal expertise."

## Copy review checklist

Before publishing any copy, verify:

- ☐ Headlines are brief — adjectives cut, padding removed.
- ☐ All copy is sentence case (except proper nouns).
- ☐ No exclamation points, ellipses, semicolons, or emoji.
- ☐ Active voice used throughout.
- ☐ No weasel words ("may," "might," "could," "potentially").
- ☐ No hype words ("revolutionary," "cutting-edge," "game-changing").
- ☐ Periods on all fragments, em dashes for asides.
- ☐ Numbers are numerals, not words.
- ☐ Button copy is action verb + object, 1–4 words.
- ☐ Subheads explain mechanism, not hype the benefit.
- ☐ Vocabulary lock from `pl_brand_brief.md` §5 is honored (testing engineer, Claude agent, ATK, Testor, Playwright before Cypress, nearshore).
- ☐ Nothing from `pl_brand_brief.md` §6 ("What NOT to Say") appears.
- ☐ Copy scans well on mobile (short lines, clear structure).
