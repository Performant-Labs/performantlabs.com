# Performant Labs — Brand Brief (Reverse-Engineered)

*Synthesized from the Phase 3 content briefs (Homepage, About Us, Services) on 2026-04-26.*

## 1. Core Identity & Positioning
- **Who we are:** Performant Labs. Led by André Angelantoni (19+ years in Drupal).
- **What we do:** We test Drupal sites. We wrote the tools the Drupal community uses (ATK, Testor) and we're building CTRFHub for the broader testing community. We extend those tools with AI (Claude agents) that autonomously heal broken tests in CI. We also embed senior testing engineers alongside client teams.
- **What we are NOT:** We are **not** a Drupal build shop. We do not build sites; we test the ones you already have. We are not an offshore body-shop.

## 2. Target Audience (ICP)
- **Primary Persona:** Organizations running business-critical Drupal sites who need release confidence without standing up their own testing org.
- **Roles:** Agency COOs, Enterprise Engineering Directors, Agency Principals, Government/Public-Sector Procurement.
- **Mindset:** They already chose Drupal. They are likely dealing with broken, flaky, or abandoned test suites (Playwright/Cypress) and need a senior partner to take them over.

## 3. Tone of Voice
- **Pragmatic & Sober:** No hype, no fluff, no "revolutionary AI" claims. 
- **Peer-to-Peer:** Write like an engineer introducing another engineer. 
- **Specifics over Adjectives:** Use concrete facts (e.g., *"Maintainer of Automated Testing Kit on drupal.org"*) instead of vague praise (*"industry-leading testing expertise"*).
- **The "Eye-Roll" Test:** Every sentence must pass the jaded-engineer eye-roll test.
- **Point of View:** First-person plural ("we") for the company, but André is the only publicly named individual. No origin myths or "our mission is to..." framings.

## 4. Key Messaging Pillars
1. **Tools:** We wrote the open-source tools the Drupal community uses (ATK and Testor). We maintain them. We're also building **CTRFHub** — a test reporting and analytics platform built exclusively around CTRF (Common Test Report Format). CTRF-native by design, modern architecture from scratch, AI features included. In active development; public progress is documented in blog posts on this site.
2. **AI / Dogfooding:** Our tests heal themselves. Claude agents diagnose and fix broken tests in CI, and we run this exact workflow nightly on our own site.
3. **People:** We embed senior testing engineers. Nearshore capacity is available for scale, but it is always senior and Drupal-literate.

## 5. Editorial Rules & Vocabulary
- **Frameworks:** Always list *Playwright* before *Cypress*.
- **Role Naming:** Use *"Testing engineer"* (Never "QA engineer," never "tester").
- **AI Naming:** Use *"Claude agent(s)"* when referring to the autonomous-healing workflow (Not "our AI").
- **Product Naming:** *Automated Testing Kit* (first mention), then *ATK*. *Testor* (capitalized, no machine-name styling). *CTRFHub* (one word, exactly capitalized; never *CTRF Hub*, *ctrfhub*, or *Ctrf-hub*). On first mention of the underlying schema use *CTRF (Common Test Report Format)*; thereafter just *CTRF*.
- **Nearshore vs. Offshore:** Use *"nearshore"*. Never say "offshore." Never frame it around cost-savings or cheap rates.

## 6. What NOT to Say (Guardrails)
- Do not use build-shop framing ("full-stack Drupal development," "site builds").
- Do not inflate headcount ("our team of 50 engineers"). Keep it to "a small team of senior testing engineers."
- No generic Drupal evangelism ("Why Drupal?"). The audience already uses it.
- No generic "passionate about quality" language.
- No client logo walls on the About page (belongs on the Homepage).
- No GitHub star counts or install counts unless specifically verified and impressive.
- No competitor comparisons for CTRFHub. Never describe CTRFHub as "like Report Portal" or by reference to any other named test-reporting product. Describe it on its own terms — what it ingests (CTRF), how it's built (CTRF-native, modern architecture), and what it does (analytics, AI features).
